Position

The amount of a security either owned (which constitutes a long position) or borrowed (which constitutes a short position) by an individual or by a dealer. In other words, it's a trade an investor currently holds open.

For example, you might hear it used in the following contexts: 1. Dealers often take long positions in specific securities to maintain inventories and allow for quick and easy trading. 2. The trader closed his position and locked in a profit of 10%.
