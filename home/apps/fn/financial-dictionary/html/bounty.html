Bounty

  • A generous amount of something.
  • A reward for capturing or even killing an undesirable person.
  • A sum paid by a government to reward certain activities or behavior, such as reaching a predefined economic goal or performing a public service.
  • An abundance of fresh produce, for example, is commonly referred to as "the season's bounty" or "nature's bounty."
  • Someone who makes a living by locating wanted persons is called a bounty hunter. A bounty hunter might track down someone who skips bail and get paid a percentage of the bail when they catch the criminal.
  • Some governments might offer a bounty to an individual who enlists in that country's armed forces (this is illegal in the United States, however).
