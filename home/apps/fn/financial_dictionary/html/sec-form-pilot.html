SEC Form PILOT

A form that is filed with the SEC by self-regulatory organizations (SRO) as an initial operation report or an amendment to an initial operation report, or a quarterly report for pilot trading systems operated by self-regulatory organizations. An SRO is a non-governmental body with regulatory authority over an industry, such as FINRA - the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.

A pilot trading system means any trading system operated by a SRO that: (a) has been in operation for less than two years (b) is independent of any other trading system operated by an SRO (c) has traded no more than 5% (or 1%) of the average daily volume of a U.S. security during at least two of the last four consecutive months (d) during two of the last four consecutive months has traded no more than 20% of the average trading volume of all the trading systems operated by the SRO.
